## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This Strategic Blueprint outlines a clear, community-centered roadmap to transform Kaga Local Government into a model of inclusive development and accountable governance. Developed through broad consultations with traditional leaders, academicians, student unions, and grassroots stakeholders, the plan reflects the shared aspirations of the people of Kaga.

While acknowledging the remarkable progress made under the leadership of His Excellency, Prof. Babagana Umara Zulum CON, Mni, FNSE, The Executive governor of Borno State, particularly in Education and Human Capital development, Agriculture, Healthcare, Good Governance, Security and infrastructure, the document identifies remaining gaps and proposes targeted actions to address them.

These interventions are organized under **six (6) priority pillars**:
- Education and Human Capital development
- Youth and Women Empowerment
- Healthcare and Environmental sanitation
- Agriculture and rural economy
- Peace and security
- Infrastructure and Information and Communication Technology (ICT) Development

Fully aligned with Borno State's 25-Year Development Framework and 10-Year Strategic Transformation Plan, the blueprint aims to leverage state-level initiatives and external support for broader impact. It also recommends a baseline needs assessment focused on the six (6) core pillars to guide evidence-based implementation.

## 2. INTRODUCTION

Kaga Local Government stands at the crossroads of transformation and opportunity. With its rich cultural heritage, resilient communities, and strategic location, Kaga has the potential to become a beacon of inclusive development in Borno State.

Under the purposeful leadership of His Excellency, Professor Babagana Umara Zulum, Kaga Local Government has witnessed significant investments across critical sectors. Road rehabilitation, expansion of healthcare services, revitalization of schools, and youth empowerment initiatives have brought tangible improvements to local communities.

This blueprint builds upon these commendable achievements by proposing strategic support in areas that still require attention. By aligning closely with the state government, we aim to ensure every ward benefits from Kaga's continued transformation.

This document serves as a strategic guide, a forward-thinking roadmap that outlines practical, measurable, and community-driven development interventions. It reflects unwavering commitment to servant-leadership and responsible governance.

## 4. OVERVIEW OF KAGA LOCAL GOVERNMENT

### Geographic Profile
- **Location**: Northern part of Borno State
- **Administrative Headquarters**: Benisheikh
- **Strategic Position**: Along Maiduguri–Damaturu highway
- **Landmass**: Approximately 2,700 square kilometers
- **Population**: Over 180,000 residents (recent projections)

### Administrative Structure
- **Wards**: 15 wards
- **Predominant Ethnic Group**: Kanuri
- **Economic Base**: Predominantly agrarian

### Economic Characteristics
- **Primary Activities**: Farming, animal husbandry, small-scale commerce
- **Agricultural Production**: Grains, vegetables, livestock
- **Market Reach**: Supplies markets across Borno and neighboring states

## 8. PRIORITY DEVELOPMENT AGENDA

This section outlines the actionable programs and development priorities that will form the cornerstone of governance in Kaga Local Government. Each sub-sector has been selected based on its immediate impact, potential for sustainability, and relevance to community needs.

### Core Development Pillars

1. **Education and Human Capital Development**
2. **Youth and Women Empowerment**
3. **Health and Environmental Sanitation**
4. **Agriculture and Rural Development**
5. **Peacebuilding and Security Collaboration**
6. **Infrastructure and ICT Development**

---

## PILLAR 1: EDUCATION AND HUMAN CAPITAL DEVELOPMENT

### Goal
Ensure equitable access to quality education and skills development across all wards in Kaga, enabling inclusive growth and a knowledge-driven society.

### Key Interventions

#### School Infrastructure & Equipment
- Rehabilitate and equip public primary and secondary schools with modern learning tools
- Promote digital literacy in schools

#### Teacher Support & Development
- Provide incentives to outstanding teachers and educational volunteers
- Create an Education Council to promote community involvement in education

#### Student Support Programs
- Set up an Education Trust Fund to offer scholarships and support students in higher institutions, especially in STEM fields
- Offer free JAMB registration to indigenous students
- Collaborate with Consortium of MDA's and NGOs to support indigenous students for Scholarship Programs/Opportunities

#### Specialized Programs
- Launch adult literacy programs targeting women and out-of-school youth
- Reintegrate school dropouts and support them to rewrite SSCE examinations
- Enable further education and qualification for employment opportunities

---

## PILLAR 2: YOUTH AND WOMEN EMPOWERMENT

### Goal
Promote self-reliance, productivity, and leadership through vocational training, entrepreneurship support, and targeted empowerment programs.

### Key Interventions

#### Skills Development
- Revitalize Kaga Vocational Skill Center with focus on:
  - ICT
  - Agriculture
  - Crafts
  - Entrepreneurship
- Partner with skilled artisans to serve as community-based trainers

#### Entrepreneurship Support
- Introduce the Kaga Enterprise Grant for young entrepreneurs and startups
- Establish Women and Youth Empowerment Centers for training and mentorship

#### Leadership & Governance
- Promote inclusive governance through increased youth and women participation
- Host strategic human capacity-building programs to turn job seekers into job creators

---

## PILLAR 3: HEALTH AND ENVIRONMENTAL SANITATION

### Goal
Strengthen healthcare systems and promote a clean environment to improve overall well-being and public health outcomes.

### Key Interventions

#### Healthcare Infrastructure
- Revitalize primary Health Care centres
- Improve access to clean water and WASH, particularly in rural health centers

#### Environmental Programs
- Institutionalize monthly sanitation programs modeled after Borno State monthly environmental sanitation policy

#### Public Health Education
- Launch anti-drug abuse education and prevention campaigns across communities

---

## PILLAR 4: AGRICULTURE AND RURAL ECONOMY

### Goal
Boost food production, rural livelihoods, and economic resilience through modernized agriculture and value chain development.

### Key Interventions

#### Modernization & Support
- Establish mechanized farming clusters and introduce support schemes
- Distribute improved seeds, fertilizers, and farm equipment

#### Farmer Organization & Support
- Train and support farmers' cooperatives with access to markets and finance

#### Livestock Development
- Strengthen veterinary services and livestock production programs
- Distribute livestock to women to support household income and food security

---

## PILLAR 5: PEACEBUILDING AND SECURITY COLLABORATION

### Goal
Foster peaceful coexistence, social cohesion, and grassroots security through inclusive local mechanisms.

### Key Interventions

#### Community Security Systems
- Strengthen Ward Peace Committees and mediation systems
- Strengthen collaboration with vigilantes, traditional rulers, and security forces

#### Dialogue & Engagement
- Hold town hall forums and community dialogues to promote peaceful coexistence
- Recognize and reward communities that meet security benchmarks

#### Capacity Building
- Capacity Building of Local Securities (Vigilantes, Hunters, CJTF's and Volunteers)

---

## PILLAR 6: INFRASTRUCTURE AND ICT DEVELOPMENT

### Goal
Enhance physical connectivity and digital inclusion to support socio-economic development and government efficiency.

### Key Interventions

#### Physical Infrastructure
- Rehabilitate rural and township roads to connect economic centers
- Install solar-powered streetlights in towns, markets, and public areas
- Drill boreholes in underserved areas to ensure clean water access

#### Digital Infrastructure
- Revitalize the existing ICT centre and establish a JAMB CBT center
- Digitize local governance processes for transparency and efficiency

## 9. IMPLEMENTATION & MONITORING FRAMEWORK

To guide the successful rollout of this blueprint, a preliminary needs assessment will be conducted across the Local Government focusing on the six (6) core Pillars.

### Framework Components

#### 1. Participatory Planning
- Engage community stakeholders, ward representatives, and traditional institutions
- Identify local needs and set priorities

#### 2. Project Implementation Units
- Establish implementation teams within the Local Government secretariat
- Oversee and coordinate the six (6) core pillars

#### 3. Performance Indicators
- Define clear performance metrics for every initiative
- Track outputs, outcomes, and impact

#### 4. Quarterly Progress Reports
- Publish public updates and scorecards
- Inform citizens of milestones achieved and challenges encountered

#### 5. Monitoring and Evaluation Committee (MEC)
- Create a dedicated team responsible for:
  - Periodic audits
  - Field inspections
  - Data verification

#### 6. Partnership Engagement
- Leverage support from Borno state government ministries
- Engage development partners and NGOs for technical and financial support

#### 7. Feedback Mechanisms
- Set up accessible platforms for citizens to:
  - Provide input
  - Report issues
  - Participate in local governance processes
